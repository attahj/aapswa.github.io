Who We Are

Afghan Association
of Puget Sound

The Afghan Association of Puget Sound represents thousands of Afghan families who have made the greater Seattle area their home. From longtime residents to recently resettled families, our community is vibrant, resilient, and deeply committed to one another.

For years, we have gathered in homes, parks, and borrowed halls. Now, we are working toward something permanent — a dedicated space where every Afghan family in our region can find community, support, and belonging.

Community founded

Community First

We are a grassroots organization founded by Afghan families, for Afghan families — rooted in the belief that belonging is built together.

Cultural Preservation

Our center will be a living space for Afghan language, arts, traditions, and heritage — ensuring future generations stay connected to their roots.

Bridging Two Worlds

We help newly arrived Afghans navigate American life while celebrating the rich culture and values we bring to the Pacific Northwest.

Our Vision

The Afghan Community Center

We are planning and fundraising for a permanent, purpose-built community center to serve Afghan families across the greater North Seattle and South Snohomish County region. This will be a place of gathering, learning, healing, and joy.

Education & Tutoring

After-school programs, language classes in Dari and Pashto, and adult literacy support.

Community Dining Hall

A gathering space for Eid celebrations, Nowruz, weddings, and weekly community meals.

Cultural Arts Center

Space for traditional Afghan music, dance, storytelling, and visual arts exhibitions.

Career & Business Services

Job placement assistance, small business support, and professional development workshops.

Youth & Family Programs

Childcare, youth sports, mentorship programs, and family counseling services.

Resettlement Support

Assistance navigating housing, healthcare, legal resources, and government services.

Target: $2,500,000

We are raising funds to purchase and develop a facility of approximately 10,000–15,000 sq ft in the North Seattle / South Snohomish County corridor. Every dollar brings us closer to breaking ground.
